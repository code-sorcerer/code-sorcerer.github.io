AI in games


I've always wondered: why are NPCs in video games so dumb? This is one of the top complaints about every other game and a constant source of jokes between fellow Sorcerers. Off the top of my head I can name only one paper about AI in games - that is about AI of a shooter game F.E.A.R. More than 10 years have passed, but F.E.A.R.'s AI is still considered the best in FPS by some, and I've never heard about any other game's outstanding AI either.

Is it hard to code or is it simple, but game design becomes hard with intelligent NPCs, and they're generally not needed for a good game?

There are arguments in support of the latter - for example in horror games like Amnesia monsters certainly should not be that clever if at all. Very simple behavior and a couple of designer tricks are enough to terrify the player and build immersion. You can exploit player's imagination even without a single "physical" NPC in your simulated world. A great game surely can be made based on great story, fun gameplay or it can be online - there is no need for intelligent NPCs when you have human characters all around, right?

On the other hand, maybe good AI is hard to develop and not so many programmers can actually do it? Well, if we're talking about Deep Learning AI - that is hard without a doubt. F.E.A.R. guys didn't employ deep neural net-augmented soldiers, they used a much simpler algorithm. And it seems there are many simple ideas one can try!

As I'm neither a Story Sorcerer, nor Game Design Sorcerer, but I want some gamedev fun too, I've decided to explore the possibility of conjuring intelligent NPCs for a simple game. So be it:

In this blog series we'll code intelligent NPCs for our new nextgen text-based RPG.
